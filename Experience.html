Team Lead

Experience with E-Cell Team as Team Lead During my tenure as the E-Cell Team Lead, I had the privilege of leading a dynamic team of 18 members in the National Entrepreneurship Challenge (NEC) organized by IIT Bombay. Our journey started when our TPO (Training and Placement Officer) notified us via a student group message about a meeting the next day at 4:30 PM at the TP Seminar Hall. My classmates and I attended, where the TPO presented us with the choice of pursuing a startup or a job and introduced us to NEC, a prestigious 6-month competition with significant monetary rewards Following the meeting, we were asked to introduce ourselves, which led to the formation of our team. I was honored to be appointed as the team lead and instructed to report at 9 AM the next day to define our roles. With only 2 days left to complete our tasks, we had to address 6 to 10 tasks in a high-pressure environment, working from 9 AM to 6:30 PM. As team lead, I was responsible for managing updates, monitoring emails, and tracking task progress. Balancing this role with my internship and participation in the Smart India Hackathon 2023, I developed crucial skills in leadership, task management, and effective communication. This experience taught me the importance of staying calm, being patient, and fostering teamwork Our hard work paid off as we achieved an all-India rank of 31 within 6 months. We later shared our experience with the college community and organized a successful post-event gathering. The positive feedback from juniors underscored our dedication. This journey highlighted the significance of respecting each team member and understanding that building a strong team is a gradual process. I learned to inspire, encourage, and uplift my teammates, ensuring everyone had the opportunity to contribute and grow. Overall, this experience was a profound learning curve in leadership and team management, and I am proud of our team's achievements.

Inten at Vinz Global

Firstly, I was really happy when our TPO shared that five of us were selected for the internship out of 25 people. I was able to secure my seat. Then, we were informed that the date was October 16, 2023, and I was delighted to learn that my mentor was Krunal, sir. He made me comfortable with all the IT stuff and gradually updated me. I feel that having the right mentor for our career is important, and I found that in him. I started with DBMS management system. As the exam approached, we decided to take a break. In January 2023, we started again, but now the game changed; as there were five of us, they formed us into a team and provided us with the CV fraud detection system.

Firstly, we had to include all the colleges in India in our search. Users could type in "IIT" or "Indian Institute," and we had to conduct the search accordingly. This took a little more time because the technologies mentioned were Java, Spring Boot, and for frontend, ReactJS, which were new to us. But as time went on, we were able to extract the email IDs, phone numbers, and other information from the resumes. However, some resume formats were different, so it took 2-3 days to figure out how to extract the names from them. Regarding phone numbers, there were 8 to 9 different formats, so we used regular expressions. Eventually, we were able to extract names, phone numbers, email IDs, college names, skills, and experiences. This is still a work in progress and will continue to grow.
